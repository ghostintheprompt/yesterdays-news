# Thesis

## Core Idea

The market does not care that you finally understand the story.

By the time the story feels clean enough to explain, the tape may already be done with it.

That is the foundation of `Yesterday's News`.

## Problem

Finance media, social chatter, and even internal analysis often confuse:

- narrative clarity
- with tradable timing

Those are not the same thing.

A market story can be:

- true
- well written
- intelligent
- deeply researched

and still be useless as a trade.

## Practical Use

This repo is for asking:

- How late was the publication relative to the event?
- How much of the move happened before the write-up?
- Was there anything genuinely novel in the piece?
- Did the tape front-run the narrative?
- Is the commentary adding edge or just adding explanation?

## Why This Matters

This matters for:

- traders
- researchers
- market commentators
- anyone building finance-related AI tools

Because a model trained to summarize news can still fail the real test:

did it help before the move died?

## Long-Term Direction

The repo can evolve into:

- latency scoring for articles and alerts
- a research tool for media lag
- an evaluator for AI-generated market commentary
- a dashboard that shows story freshness relative to price action

That is a useful wedge because it avoids pretending to predict the future.
It focuses on something more honest:

whether the thing in front of you is already stale.
