# Thesis

## Core Idea

If it is public, it is late.

That does not make it worthless.
It makes it late.

By the time the story feels clean enough to explain, the trade may already be done with it.

That is the foundation of `Yesterday's News`.

Public information is usually not edge.
It is usually a receipt, a concept, an observation, or publicity.

## Problem

Finance media, social chatter, company PR, and even internal analysis often confuse:

- narrative clarity
- with tradable timing

Those are not the same thing.

A market story can be:

- true
- well written
- intelligent
- deeply researched

and still be useless as a trade.

That is the insult buried inside the title.
Yesterday's news can be excellent journalism, decent analysis, useful PR, and still completely dead money.

## Practical Use

This repo is for asking:

- How late was the publication relative to the event?
- How much of the move happened before the write-up?
- Was there anything genuinely novel in the piece?
- Did the tape front-run the narrative?
- Is the commentary adding edge or just adding explanation?
- If it is late, is it still useful as concept, observation, or publicity?
- Is this a signal, or just a guy on the phone hoping confidence can outrun lateness?
- Which sources are habitually early, and which ones mainly publish receipts?

## Why This Matters

This matters for:

- traders
- researchers
- market commentators
- anyone building finance-related AI tools
- anyone evaluating or red-teaming AI systems that produce market commentary

Because a model trained to summarize news can still fail the real test:

did it help before the move died?

That question matters for humans too.
There is a huge amount of finance content that exists to explain the move after the tape has already buried the opportunity.

This is also why finance is a good stress test for AI evaluation.
A model can sound brilliant, be factually correct, and still fail the only question that mattered in the room.

## Long-Term Direction

The repo can evolve into:

- latency scoring for articles and alerts
- a research tool for media lag
- an evaluator for AI-generated market commentary
- a red-team surface for polished-but-late financial output
- a terminal-native workflow for judging whether a market story is alive, late, useful, or already buried

That is a useful wedge because it avoids pretending to predict the future.
It focuses on something more honest:

whether the thing in front of you is already stale, and if it is, what kind of value is still left.
